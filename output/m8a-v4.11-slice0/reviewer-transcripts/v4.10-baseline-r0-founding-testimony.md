## Verbatim Complaint

> Translated from the original Chinese-language reviewer prompt; meaning preserved verbatim.

```text
Please first take a careful look at @scenario-globe-viewer.
This project was built to meet @itri's requirements, and a specific
route has already been developed that you can reach via the icon in
the top-right corner. The current view still has a lot of pieces
that need adjusting. For example, the details panel right now is
packed with data, but I have absolutely no idea what it's doing -
and what is the truth feature actually for? And why can't I open
both at the same time? We should be rethinking which pieces of
information could actually be presented via hover popovers or
toasts. But the hover popover that comes up when I point at a
satellite is also a bit too large right now - it often blocks the
satellite across the whole view, so that seems to need adjusting
too? Some pieces of information, if the volume isn't very large
and they don't need to stay on screen for very long, could also be
considered for placement near the satellite or in some other
appropriate position - rather than all being crammed into the
details block, where the information is unreadable and far too
concentrated. See if we need to draft a new SDD plan covering
what the entire frontend cross-orbit satellite handover should
present and how it should be presented, so that the whole
frontend really feels like a product rather than just work being
turned in to get it done. Would using the ui-ux-pro-max skill help?
Please find a way to gather resources, and you also need to look
at this entire view from the user's perspective and think about
what kind of presentation is genuinely meaningful and valuable.
Information isn't enough just by appearing - how it's presented
is extremely important. This whole project should already have
collected a huge amount of real-world cross-orbit satellite
communication data - is that being shown right now? Or is that
also being stuffed inside details, where I can't see at all
which pieces are the truly important ones?
```
